The acquisition of some of lender Banco Master's assets by Brazil's government-controlled bank BRB is likely to exclude "much more" than 23 billion reais ($3.89 billion) in assets, BRB's president said on Thursday.  BRB is still analyzing Banco Master and the final price for its assets has not been defined, although it is expected to decrease from earlier indications, President Paulo Henrique Costa said at a press conference, after the bank released fourth-quarter results.  "Due diligence at this moment is focused on the credit portfolio," he said, adding that only a portion of the credit operations would be considered part of the business to be analyzed by the Brazilian central bank.
