The company has secured over one million square feet of manufacturing space to support its domestic operations, including facilities in Arizona for chip production and Texas for building AI supercomputers. Shares of Nvidia rose more than 3.5% in premarket trading following the announcement. Nvidia is partnering with Amkor Technology (NASDAQ:AMKR) and SPIL for packaging and testing work in Arizona. Founder and CEO Jensen Huang said the move enhances supply chain strength and improves the company's ability to meet increasing demand for AI infrastructure. Production of the Blackwell GPUs has already begun at Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing's (NYSE:TSM) fabrication facility in Phoenix. Meanwhile, Nvidia is working with Foxconn on supercomputer production in Houston and with Wistron on similar efforts in Dallas.
